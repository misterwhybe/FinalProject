General information

Life Ladder

This webpage shows the average score of the life ladder per country. What is a life ladder? According to the World Happiness Report, a life ladder is used when respondents are asked to think of a ladder, with the best possible life for them being a 10, and the worst possible life being a 0. They are then asked to rate their own current lives on that 0 to 10 scale. With a life ladder we can see how residents experience their own life.
keep in mind: this is subjective and we can never be sure if these numbers are representative.

Drugs Deaths

The colours represent a certain average. Click a country and a bar chart will show. Here, you can see the average of that specific country over the last years. If a certain year doesn't have a bar, there wasn't any data from this year. Hover over the bar to see it's value. After you clicked a country, a pie chart will also show. Here, you can see how many people died in that country as a result of drugs and alcohol. Use the slider next to it to select the year you want to see.

Bar Chart

A bar chart consists of several bars. Here, it contains the average score for the life ladder. It only shows one country, the country you clicked on. If you click another country, this bar chart will go away and a new one will take its place. It shows the years 2005-2016. Sometimes, a certain year is empty. This means there is no data available for this year. The colour of the bars is the same as the legend of the worldmap, so the higher the score the higher the score (from lightgreen to darkgreen)

Pie Chart

After a country is clicked, a pie chart will be shown on the right. This pie chart shows us the amount of deaths in that country which are related to drugs and alcohol. A slider is available under the world map. Use this slider to select which year you want to see. The standard year is 1990. Take the slider and move it!
