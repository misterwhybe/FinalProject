General information

Life Ladder

This webpage shows the average score of the life ladder per country. What is a life ladder? According to the World Happiness Report, a life ladder is used when respondents are asked to think of a ladder, with the best possible life for them being a 10, and the worst possible life being a 0. They are then asked to rate their own current lives on that 0 to 10 scale. With a life ladder we can see how residents experience their own life. Hover over a country to see the average life ladder.
Keep in mind: this is subjective and we can never be sure if these numbers are representative.

Drug related Deaths

After you click on a country, a pie chart will also be shown. This pie chart will show how many people died in that specified country related to drugs and alcohol. Click the button to go to the source. The problem with this dataset is that it contains the amount of deaths per year per country, and the numbers have a lot of decimals. You would think that the deaths will be a whole number, and not a float. Therefore, the number shown is rounded.

Bar Chart

A bar chart consists of several bars. Here, it contains the average score for the life ladder. It only shows the one country you clicked on. If you click another country, this bar chart will disappear and a new one will take its place. It shows the years 2005-2016. A missing bar means there is no data available for this year. The colour of the bars is the same as the worldmap legend, so the denser the colour the higher the score (ranging from lightgreen to darkgreen). Hover over a bar and it will show it's value!

Pie Chart

After a country is clicked, a pie chart will be shown on the right. This pie chart shows the amount of deaths in that country which are related to drugs and alcohol. A slider is available under the world map. Use this slider to select which year you want to see. The standard year is 1990. Take the slider and move it!
